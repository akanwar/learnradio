First Light
Codename: Recon · The Patron’s Mouse
ObjectiveStand in front of the machine that started it all, and meet the patron of the whole summer.

Briefing

Before you build anything, you need to know whose footsteps you’re following. Claude Shannon figured out the mathematics of every message ever sent — he gave the world the bit. During WWII he met Alan Turing, the Bletchley codebreaker, and that conversation launched Shannon’s work on secret codes. He also built playful machines, including Theseus, a mechanical mouse that learns a maze and remembers the way out. Its “brain” is just telephone relay switches. The original is on display at the MIT Museum — ten minutes from home, in the institute your Handler attended.

Field Operations

  1. Travel to the MIT Museum in Kendall Square. Find Theseus.
  2. Watch how it “remembers” a path it has already solved. Ask: where is the memory actually stored?
  3. Handler’s briefing: hear the story the labels don’t tell — the bit, the Turing meeting, why a master’s thesis changed the world.
  4. Make the connection out loud: the Bombe you saw at Bletchley and this mouse are linked by one real wartime friendship.
  5. Debrief over food. Sketch how a relay works from memory.

Equipment

  • Field notebook + pencil
  • Museum admission
  • One curious agent

Debrief — mission complete when

  • You can explain what a relay does
  • You can say why Theseus “remembers”
  • You can link the Bombe to the mouse
Handler note: No gadgets yet on purpose. This mission is about awe — the feeling that the whole summer is one continuous story you’re about to join.
